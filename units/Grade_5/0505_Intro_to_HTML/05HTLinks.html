Teaching Point:

Today we will learn how to Add Links to Other Pages in our HTML.

Links

Links are buttons on your HTML page that, when pressed, open up another webpage. It might be another page in your website, or another website altogether. To add links in HTML, we use the "a" tag. Like the img tag, the "a" tag uses ATTRIBUTES to tell the web browser what page to open up when it is pressed.

The link that people click on should go in between the two "a" tags. Writing or a picture can be used as a link. Here is an example of an a tag that uses the href attribute to say what page to go to. In this example the link is the words "click here to go to apple.com". The href attribute tells the web browser to make this a link to the apple website.

Today's Assignment

Make a links section on your webpage. Use a title tag to write Useful Sites, and include 3 or more links to other webpages about your topic. Don't forget to use "http://" or "https://" at the beginning of your href.

Extension Activity

Use the target="_blank" attribute to make your webpage open in a new window or tab.
